# Code of Conduct

SeaLegs follows a simple standard: be respectful, practical, and clear.

## Expected Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect different levels of experience with Swift, macOS, gaming, and
  accessibility tooling.
- Keep feedback specific to the work.
- Assume privacy and safety concerns are valid until verified otherwise.
- Accept maintainers' decisions when scope, safety, or maintenance cost is the
  reason for saying no.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Posting sensitive screenshots, logs, or diagnostic files from another user.
- Repeated off-topic arguments after a maintainer has closed the discussion.
- Pressuring maintainers to ship risky screen-capture, input-monitoring, or
  security-sensitive behavior.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or delete comments and issues that violate
this code of conduct. Maintainers may also block users from the project when
necessary to protect contributors and users.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainer through an
available private contact channel listed on the GitHub profile. Do not open a
public issue containing personal or sensitive details.
